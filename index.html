04 — Writing
Nov 2025
Robust Fall Detection Across Camera Viewpoints — VIRA-GCN (Sensors, MDPI)
A common failure mode of skeleton-based fall detection models: they work well on the camera angle they were trained on, then fall apart at a different perspective or with partial occlusion. This paper introduces VIRA-GCN — an adaptation of RA-GCNv2 — with two practical fixes: a synthetic viewpoint augmentation pipeline that generates rotated and occluded training sequences without additional hardware, and a consensus-based inference-time aggregation over multiple synthetic views. Result: 99.81% accuracy on Le2i, 7.50ms inference, 4M parameters. Generalises across datasets via a per-joint affine transformation from Kinect to MMPose format.
Peer-reviewed · Sensors (MDPI) · Skeleton-based CV · Co-author
Nov 2017
Location-aware kernels for large image tiled processing
Working on OCR of large noisy images, I needed CNNs to know where in the global image each tile was being processed. The solution — injecting explicit spatial coordinates as extra channels — was developed independently while building an operational system, eight months before Uber AI Labs published the same idea as CoordConv at NeurIPS 2018. The right answer to a real problem, found twice.

Automatic evaluation of image alignment accuracy
When you have thousands of aligned face or document images and can't annotate them all, how do you measure alignment quality without ground truth? A pragmatic approach using local template matching and IoU against expected feature positions — with a semi-supervised bonus if quality is high enough.
